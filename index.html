What Happened — Day by Day
10 March — Day 1
Rushed to ER with SpO₂ at 66%. Started on Non-Invasive Ventilation (NIV/BiPAP). Shifted to MICU 1. PCO₂ dangerously high (84 mmHg), pH 7.24 — severe respiratory acidosis.
11 March — Day 2
NIV failure overnight. PCO₂ crossed 115 with pH dropping to 7.06 — life-threatening. Emergency intubation at 9:00 AM. Placed on mechanical ventilator. Also treated for high potassium (6.0 mEq/L).
12 March — Day 3
Shifted from PCV to PSV mode (lighter ventilator support). Abdomen ultrasound done. PCO₂ improving. Stable on ventilator. Plan made to attempt extubation.
13 March — Day 4
Successfully extubated (breathing tube removed) at 3:30 PM. Tolerating intermittent NIV well. Shifted to MICU 3. Kidney function improving. Afebrile.
14 March — Today ✓
Still in ICU but danger has passed. On intermittent BiPAP support. Alert, following commands. Doctors advise BiPAP machine at home for nightly use after discharge.
